Cause if we can't protect the Earth, you can be damn sure we'll avenge it

The Marvel Cinematic Universe MCU is shared universe that is centered on a series of superhero films. Check out our up-to-date list of Marvel Avengers films below.

The Avengers are a team of extraordinary individuals, either with superpowers or other special characteristics. Its purpose is to protect global stability from inner or extraterrestrial threats.

The original team consisted of (from left to right) Nick Fury, Hawkeye, Hulk, Iron Man, Captain America, Thor and Black Widow in The Avengers and Avengers: Age of Ultron, and shifted when Tony Stark, Thor, Clint Barton, and Bruce Banner/Hulk left the team after the events of Avengers: Age of Ultron.

For more information about the ever-expanding Avengers team check out Marvel.com.

Meet the Avengers

Nick Fury

Nicholas "Nick" Joseph Fury, portrayed by Samuel L. Jackson and first appearing in the 2008 film Iron Man, was a former Colonel who had served with the United States Army and an espionage veteran of the CIA during the Cold War.

Continuing to serve the world, Fury joined S.H.I.E.L.D., a covert international peacekeeping organization which operates as humanity's first line of defense against Earth's most dangerous enemies.

Later promoted to Director of S.H.I.E.L.D., Fury refocused his efforts to organize the Avengers Initiative.


Hawkeye

An expert marksman and fighter, Clint Barton puts his talents to good use by working for S.H.I.E.L.D. as a special agent. The archer known as Hawkeye also boasts a strong moral compass that at times leads him astray from his direct orders.

Barton became one of the best agents of S.H.I.E.L.D., responsible for the recruitment of Black Widow, whom he developed a strong friendship with.


Hulk

Robert Bruce Banner, M.D., Ph.D., is a renowned scientist and a founding member of the Avengers.

Highly respected for his work in biochemistry, nuclear physics, and gamma radiation, he was commissioned to recreate the Super Soldier Serum which created Captain America.

After being exposed to heavy doses of gamma radiation, scientist Bruce Banner transforms into the mean, green rage machine called the Hulk.


Ironman

Anthony Edward "Tony" Stark is a billionaire industrialist, genius, inventor, playboy and philanthropist, a founding member of the Avengers, and the former CEO of Stark Industries, a company originally started by his father, Howard Stark.

Tony Stark's confidence is only matched by his high-flying abilities as the hero called Iron Man, a suit of armer he wears while protecting the planet.

When not flying as Iron Man he applies his genius to creating high-tech solutions to global problems, like clean renewable energy.


Captain America

Captain Steven Grant "Steve" Rogers is a World War II veteran, a founding member of the Avengers, and Earth's first known superhero.

Rogers had suffered from numerous health problems, and upon America's entry into World War II, was rejected from military service despite several attempts to enlist.

Recipient of the Super Soldier Serum he fights for American ideals as one of the world’s mightiest heroes and the leader of the Avengers.


Thor

Thor Odinson is the former King of New Asgard, a founding member of the Avengers, and the God of Thunder.

When his irresponsible and impetuous behavior reignited a conflict between Asgard and Jotunheim, he was denied the right to become king, stripped of his power, and banished by his father Odin to Earth.

After redeeeming himself, he was granted his power once more, and now wields his mighty hammer Mjölnir to fight evil throughout the Nine Realms and beyond protect his home Asgard and planet Earth alike.


Black Widow

Despite super spy Natasha Romanoff’s checkered past, she’s become one of S.H.I.E.L.D.’s most deadly assassins and a frequent member of the Avengers.

Trusted by some and feared by most, the Black Widow strives to make up for the bad she had done in the past by helping the world, even if that means getting her hands dirty in the process.
